As is typical with mythological creatures and stories, there are many interpretations of the Wendigo. This name is used to describe a mythical creature, an idea, and a pycological disorder. All of these share the same characteristics: cannabalism and insatiable greed/hunger.


The common description of the mythological creature, as referenced in various media, is that of a person who resorted to cannibalism due to starvation and thereby transformed into a creature with exceptional speed and strength, a gaunt, corpse-like figure, and an insatiable hunger for human flesh.
